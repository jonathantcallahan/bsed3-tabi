PHYSICAL HEALTH
HEALTH IMPACT OF ANIMAL PRODUCTS
  • Due to high levels of saturated fats and dietary cholesterol, omnivores are 40% more likely to develop heart disease(1)
  • Some fish contain high levels of Omega 3 fatty acids which are important for healthy brain function and are difficult to source from plants
  • Omnivores are 15% more likely to develop cancer, are more likely to be obese, and are more likely to develop diabetes(3)
  • Meat and other animal products often contain antibiotics, carcinogens, and added hormones(15)(16)(2)
  • Less substantiated claims link the consumption of animal products to chronic lower respiratory disease, stroke, and kidney disease
HEALTH IMPACT OF PLANTS
Plant heavy diets naturally tend to have healthier macro ratios (fats/ carbs/ protein). Plants are high in fiber which improves digestive health (6), and also contain several other important vitamins and minerals (5). Vegan and vegetarian diets have been shown to support a healthier gut microbiome (7) which has far reaching health effects. The low calorie density of many plant-centric meals helps with losing excess weight. (8) Poorly planned vegan diets may provide insufficient amounts of essential fatty acids, vitamin B12, iron, calcium, iodine or zinc
NOTICEABLE PHYSICAL BENEFITS
Some of the more noticeable phsical benefits of a vegan diet include clearer skin (12), reduced bad breath and less body odor (11), better digestive health, and improved sexual health (14). While there is anecdotal evidence for everything from whiter teeth and strong nails to increased energy, significant studies have not been done. It is likely many of these benefits have more do with overall improvements in diet that come along with a vegan diet like less processed foods and sugar than the absence of animal products.
